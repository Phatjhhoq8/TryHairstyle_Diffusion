    With the increasing demand for personalized beauty services, virtual hairstyle try-on has emerged as a promising application for computer vision and generative AI. However, conventional Deep Learning-based inpainting techniques often struggle with background degradation, intensive hardware consumption caused by multi-model overheads, and data loss vulnerabilities during unpredictable cloud-based training sessions.
    
    This thesis presents the design and implementation of \textit{TryHairStyle}, a robust virtual hairstyle try-on system powered by the Stable Diffusion XL architecture. The study introduces several core engineering contributions: (1) An extrapolated coordinate routing and reverse-pasting algorithm (\textit{bbox Crop-and-Paste}) that guarantees 100\% preservation of the user's original background environment; (2) A Dual-Condition mechanism integrating IP-Adapter Plus with \textit{InsightFace/InstantID} embedding networks to reliably transfer hairstyles without corrupting facial identity; and (3) An isolated AI processing manager utilizing \textit{Celery Subprocesses}, paired with an instant front-end \textit{HSV Colorize} modular tool, effectively mitigating local RAM bottlenecks.
    
    Furthermore, the fine-tuning process of the massive 13-channel UNet architecture was smoothly executed on a resource-constrained cloud environment (Google Colab T4 with 15GB VRAM), heavily relying on Two-Way \textit{I/O Auto-Caching} alongside an automated continuous \textit{Checkpoint Fallback} synchronization mechanism. Implementation outcomes demonstrate that \textit{TryHairStyle} effectively synthesizes high-fidelity, photorealistic hairstyle variations while maintaining a hardened, fault-tolerant Microservices infrastructure, laying a vigorous foundation for scalable real-world deployments.
